Man in the middle Attack

A Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attack is a cybersecurity threat where an unauthorized third party intercepts and potentially alters the communication between two parties without their knowledge.

In a MitM attack, the attacker positions themselves between the communicating parties, allowing them to eavesdrop on or manipulate the data being exchanged.

This can occur through various means, such as exploiting vulnerabilities in network infrastructure or using techniques like ARP spoofing to redirect traffic through the attacker's system.

Common Forms of MitM Attacks

  • Wi-Fi Eavesdropping
  • Packet Sniffing
  • DNS Spoofing
  • SSL Stripping

Protecting Against MitM Attacks

To protect against Man-in-the-Middle attacks:

  • Use secure communication channels.
  • Employ encryption, such as HTTPS.
  • Be cautious when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks.
  • Regularly update software.
  • Consider using VPNs (Virtual Private Networks).
